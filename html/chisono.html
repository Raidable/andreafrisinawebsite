Chi Sono

I’m Andrea, born in 2000, and I’ve always felt a bit outside the usual path.

I grew up in Reggio Calabria, a city in the far south of Italy, with a passion for cinema, a passion shared by my family from the very beginning. From a young age, they brought me to explore films, stories, and perspectives, helping me fall in love with this incredible art form, the foundation from which everything I do today derives.

At just five years old, I had a small red camera in my hands, and I started taking my first photos. I don’t really know why, but I immediately felt at home behind the lens: it became my window to the world, my way to observe life and translate it visually.

Growing up in a city that can feel a bit closed, I quickly realized I needed to look beyond its limits to grow, absorb new experiences, and discover new perspectives. That drive has always been part of me: the desire to explore, to see life elsewhere, and to let my passion take me where moments are waiting.

In the early years of high school, I threw myself into creating videos on YouTube, at first just for fun. Over time, I met people who shared the same passion, bought my first cameras, and started seeing real results, even reaching 80,000 subscribers.

I closed that chapter in 2019, even though everything was going well. I felt I needed to take a step further, to explore my passion more deeply, so after high school I pursued artistic studies that had been my dream since childhood. This path led me to complete my Master’s degree in Television, Cinema, and New Media, specializing in Cinema, at IULM Milan in March 2025. It was an experience that not only honed my craft but also exposed me to life and narratives beyond my hometown.

Since then, I’ve started my freelance career, moving between projects for friends, clients, and more personal, self-funded, low-budget films. My work has taken me across Italy, experimenting with genres, but always keeping the same core goal: turning every project into a cinematic experience. I love the cinematic look, the atmosphere, the rhythm - whether it’s a client video or a short film - and that feeling is at the center of everything I do.

Alongside freelancing, I’ve gained experience in professional settings like ALMA Culinary School, La Scala Theatre in Milan, and the Ischia Film Festival, working as a videomaker and photographer. These experiences helped me understand different ways of working, expand my perspective, and discover new life fragments, while still keeping my personal cinematic vision at the heart of my work.

I’ve always had a desire to explore the world and see life beyond my hometown, and my passion has been the key to doing that. Today, I’m not tied to a single place: I move between bases, mainly Reggio Calabria and Milan, following stories wherever they take me, and letting my curiosity guide me to new experiences and narratives.
